School Identity

USM SEAL, COLORS AND MASCOT

School Seal

The Seal of the University of Southern Mindanao shall depict the shape of a "Talam", the Muslim Tray of Bounty, consisting of two (2) parts: the outer and inner circular planes.

In the outer plane shall bear the inscription, "University of Southern Mindanao" written in bold, ethnic style on top and "Kabacan, Cotabato", at the bottom, the name and principal address of the Institution, respectively.

Within the inner plane shall have the following symbolic representations:

Map of Mindanao

the background showing the extent of the service area of USM over which radiate, after forming the gear-shaped "halo" of unity and progress, the aural rays of peace and sustainable socio- economic development emanating from the glowing candles of quality and relevant education, thus exulting the immense far-reaching impact of the University in this part of the country.

Right Hand Carrying Four Candles

A right hand representing the concerted efforts of the USM administration, faculty, staff and students to pursue and uphold a culture of excellence in carrying out the four-fold functions of the University, namely:

Represents:
  • Instruction
  • Research
  • Extension
  • Production

The service of Almighty God and the nation, as symbolized by the four (4) lighted candles that it firmly holds.

Spur Gear

A spur gear depicting the "halo" that wreathes the four (4) lighted symbolic candles which exudes the resulting remarkable progress in commerce and industry in the operationalization of the a forecited mandated four- fold functions of the University that will help accelerate the socio- economic development of its service area and bring about lasting peace and harmony among its populace thereby hastening the integration of Christians, Muslim and other cultural communities therein, which is expressive of the principal mission of the institution.

Open Book

An open book symbolizing the institution's altruistic pursuit for truth and knowledge at the foreground below the right hand with inscription "MIT" representing the old name of the institution, Mindanao Institute of Technology, on its left page and USM" on its right page, which proudly illustrates its transformation into a premier of higher learning that it is today, and its relentless quest for a culture of excellence and dynamism in the performance of its four-fold function from its inception to the present.

Year 1952

An inscription 1952 below, representing the year of Our Lord the institution was first established as then Mindanao Institute of Technology, now the University of Southern Mindanao.

A pair of con leaves and panicle of palay

A pair of con leaves and panicle of palay at the left side of the book and a microscope and other laboratory equipment at the right side representing agriculture and science and technology, respectively, the two major field of specialization of the University,

School Mascot

School Mascot

The University shall adopt a mascot with the physical feature of a native animal or any creature, which the province or the region is known for, whose dominant strong character traits truly depict the ideals and aspirations the institution stands for.

Using Our Identity

Our school colors, seal, and mascot should be displayed with pride and respect. They represent the values and traditions that unite our community. Whether on uniforms, school materials, or during events, these symbols remind us of our shared commitment to excellence and our collective identity as members of this institution.
