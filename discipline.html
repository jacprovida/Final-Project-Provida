Student Conduct and Disciplines

Grounds for disciplinary actions and penalties

⚠️

This disciplinary policy is designed to maintain a safe, respectful, and productive learning environment for all students. All members of our school community are expected to uphold our standards of conduct.

Article 254. General Provisions

Students are expected to:

  • Respect all members of the school community
  • Attend all classes punctually and prepared
  • Complete assignments and homework on time
  • Follow instructions from teachers and staff
  • Maintain academic honesty and integrity
  • Dress appropriately according to uniform policy
  • Use school property and facilities responsibly
  • Refrain from bullying, harassment, or discrimination

Article 255. Norms of Conduct and Discipline

Level 1 Violations

Examples include:

  • Tardiness (arriving late to class)
  • Incomplete homework
  • Minor dress code violations
  • Using mobile devices during class

Consequences:

  • Verbal warning
  • Written reflection assignment
  • Loss of privileges (e.g., break time)
  • Parent notification after repeated offenses

Article 256. Basis of Discipline

Level 2 Violations

Examples include:

  • Repeated tardiness or truancy
  • Disruptive behavior in class
  • Disrespectful language or attitude
  • Academic dishonesty (copying homework)
  • Inappropriate use of technology

Consequences:

  • Detention (during lunch or after school)
  • Parent conference required
  • Temporary loss of privileges
  • Community service within school
  • Behavioral contract

Article 257. Ground foe Student Disciplinary Actions and Penalties

Level 3 Violations

Examples include:

  • Fighting or physical aggression
  • Bullying or harassment (verbal, physical, or cyber)
  • Cheating on exams or plagiarism
  • Vandalism of school property
  • Theft
  • Insubordination or defiance

Consequences:

  • In-school suspension (1-3 days)
  • Out-of-school suspension (depending on severity)
  • Mandatory parent meeting
  • Restitution for damaged property
  • Referral to counseling services
  • Possible loss of extracurricular participation

Article 258. Sanctions and Operative Effects

Level 4 Violations

Examples include:

  • Possession of weapons or dangerous items
  • Possession, use, or distribution of drugs or alcohol
  • Severe physical assault
  • Serious threats or intimidation
  • Illegal activities on school grounds
  • Repeated Level 3 violations

Consequences:

  • Extended suspension (5+ days)
  • Recommendation for expulsion
  • Involvement of law enforcement (if applicable)
  • Mandatory counseling or intervention program
  • Permanent record notation

Due Process

All students have the right to due process. This includes:

  • Being informed of the charges or violation
  • Having the opportunity to present their side of the story
  • Having a parent or guardian present during disciplinary meetings
  • The right to appeal decisions through proper channels

Remember: Our goal is not punishment, but education and growth. We believe in restorative practices and helping students learn from their mistakes. Students who demonstrate consistent positive behavior are recognized through our honor roll system, awards ceremonies, and special privileges.
