School History

Our proud heritage and journey

Our Foundation

Our school was founded with a vision to provide quality education that nurtures both academic excellence and character development. From humble beginnings, we have grown into a respected institution known for producing well-rounded individuals who excel in their chosen fields.

The founders believed in creating an environment where every student could discover their potential and develop the skills needed to succeed in an ever-changing world.

Timeline of Milestones

1952

Creation of MIT

June 20, 1952: Republic Act 763 is signed by President Elpidio Quirino, officially creating the Mindanao Institute of Technology (MIT).

1954

Enabling Act & Formal Opening

June 10, 1954: President Ramon Magsaysay approves Republic Act 998, the Enabling Act that authorized MIT to open officially.

October 1, 1954: Formal opening of MIT.

Kabacan Provincial High School becomes the nucleus of MIT through a Provincial Board Resolution.

The establishment was largely inspired and initiated by Bai Hadja Fatima Matabay Plang.

1955

Start of Collegiate Classes

June 1955: MIT opens with four academic departments and its first degree programs.

1976

Graduate Studies Launched

MIT begins offering graduate programs, marking a major step in academic expansion.

Metamorphosis

By virtue of Presidential Decree 1312 issued by President Ferdinand E. Marcos on March 13, 1978, the Mindanao Institute of Technology was converted into the University of Southern Mindanao through the efforts of Dr. Jaman S. Imlan The University's current academic components include the Graduate School ten colleges, one institute, the laboratory high school, autonomous campus, and the PALMA cluster campuses.

Campus Site

The University's main campus sprawls across a 1,024-hectare area, a former government estate, in Kabacan, Cotabato. The campus is ideally located in the heart of Mindanao, just 0.6 kilometers north of the national highway that traverses the town proper of Kabacan and connects the cities of Cotabato and Davao. It is about 10 kilometers west of the Matalam junction, which leads to the provinces of Sultan Kudarat and South Cotabato, as well as General Santos City, and three kilometers east of the Sayre Highway junction, which leads to Bukidnon and Cagayan de Oro City.
